# Pretty Perspectives by Style Me Pretty - Brand Voice Guide

## Publication Identity
Pretty Perspectives is Style Me Pretty's B2B publication for wedding vendors. It's the insider resource for photographers, planners, florists, venues, and other wedding professionals who want to grow their businesses.

## Voice & Tone

### Overall Character
- **Supportive mentor** - Like an experienced industry friend sharing what actually works
- **Confident but not cocky** - We know our stuff, but we're not preachy
- **Action-oriented** - Every piece should leave readers with something they can DO
- **Warm but professional** - Approachable without being fluffy

### Writing Style
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Direct, punchy sentences
- Use "you" to speak directly to vendors
- Use "we" when referring to Style Me Pretty
- Contractions are fine (don't, won't, here's)
- Avoid jargon unless explaining it

## Title Patterns

### Interview Profiles
- "[Philosophy/Trait]: [Vendor Name] on [What They Do Uniquely]"
- "Building a [Adjective]-Led Brand That Scales: Meet [Name] of [Company]"
- "How [Vendor Name] [Does Something Impressive]"
- Examples:
  - "Respect Is the Strategy: Lauren Fox on Growing Fox Events the Right Way"
  - "Building a Kindness-Led Brand That Scales: Meet Jeni Maus of Found Rental Co"

### SEO/How-To Articles
- Direct imperatives: "Stop [Doing X]. Start [Doing Y]."
- Question-based: "Why [Topic] Matters for Wedding Vendors"
- Numbered lists: "X [Things] You Need to Know for [Year]"
- Problem-solution: "The Anti-[Problem] Plan: [Solution]"
- Examples:
  - "Stop Selling Your Services. Start Solving Their Problems."
  - "The Anti-Overwhelm Plan: Systems to Protect Your Time"

### Trend/Industry Pieces
- "[Number] Wedding Trends You Need to Know for [Year]"
- "The Rise of [Topic] (and How to Respond)"
- "Gen Z vs. Millennial [Topic]: What Vendors Need to Know"

## Excerpt Patterns
- 1-2 sentences max
- End with value proposition or hook
- Use "Here's how" or similar CTAs
- For profiles: Highlight the vendor's core philosophy
- Examples:
  - "Find out what success looks like to this accomplished vendor - and what you can do to find it for yourself."
  - "By implementing a few key systems and mindset shifts, you can protect your time and get back to loving what you do. Here's how."

## Content Categories

### 1. Vendor Spotlights (Interview-based)
- Focus on their journey and philosophy
- Extract actionable advice for other vendors
- Include their perspective on industry trends
- Always mention how SMP has/could help them

### 2. SEO/Educational Content
Alternating between:
- **General business**: Marketing, pricing, client experience, systems, time management
- **Vendor-specific**: Photography tips, planner strategies, florist business, venue marketing

### 3. Industry Insights
- Trend reports
- Client behavior changes (Gen Z, millennials)
- Technology and tools
- Market shifts

## Topics to Cover (SEO Rotation)

### General Business (odd weeks)
- Marketing and visibility
- Pricing strategies
- Client communication
- Systems and automation
- Work-life balance
- Building referral networks
- Social media strategy
- Portfolio optimization

### Vendor-Specific (even weeks)
- Photography: shooting, editing, client prep, albums
- Planning: timelines, vendor management, design
- Florals: sourcing, sustainability, pricing
- Venues: marketing, packages, vendor relationships
- Catering: menus, service, dietary trends
- Videography: equipment, storytelling, delivery

## Words/Phrases to Use
- "Here's the truth..."
- "The real competitive edge"
- "What actually works"
- "Game-changer"
- "Non-negotiable"
- "Level up"
- "Stand out"
- "Book more"

## Words/Phrases to Avoid
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Wedding industry experts say..."
- Overly salesy language
- Competitor names (The Knot, WeddingWire, Zola, Brides)
- Generic business advice without wedding context
